CitiBike Web Service Project

For our Programming Paradigms final project, three classmates and I developed a RESTful JSON web service that would provide users information about CitiBike stations (e.g. how many bikes are available, what is the closest station, etc) while also giving them the ability to edit certain information. My role on the team focused on developing the Server, which was written in Python using CherryPy. Overall, working on this project was a good experience in working with a team and demonstrating many of the concepts learned in this course. The project repository can be seen here.




Databases Food Ordering App

For our Database Concepts final project, three classmates and I developed a web application in PHP that would work as an ordering platform for the food sale businesses that ran in our dormitory. Our application would save user orders in a mySQL database and then make such orders visible to the food sale businesses. The application would also make use of past order information to recommend favorite items to users. For this team, my focus was on writing the PHP and figuring out the SQL queries necessary to make this functionality possible. Working on this project provided me a great introduction to SQL and PHP.




Student International Business Council Microsoft Project

My SIBC group was tasked with presenting ideas for how Microsoft could best serve the deskless worker market. Through much research and discussion, our team believed that the best approach for Microsoft would be to use Augmented Reality to serve the manufacturing industry and IoT integration to serve the healthcare industry. My contributinos focused on the Augmented Reality portion of presentation which entailed much research on the HoloLens product and the trends of the manufacturing industry. After months of preparation, my team presented our solution to a group of Microsoft employees in Redmond, Washington. It was a great opportunity for me to practice my research, teamwork, and public speaking skills. You can see our presentation here.



Data Structure Twitter API Project

For our Data Structure Final Project, two classmates and I developed a Python program that would find relevant, connected Twitter accounts for a particular Twitter user. A custom formula was used to determine each Twitter account's value to the user and the accounts were organized using a Max Heap. Working on this project helped me learn how to work with Twitter's API and how to apply some Data Structure concepts to real world applications. The project repository can be seen here.





Student International Business Council Bosch Project

My SIBC group was tasked with providing recommendations for Bosch as they are undergoing a digital transformation. Our research led us to believe that Bosch should perform horizontal digital integration, do a combination of insourcing and creating alliances during this transformation, and invest innovation centers. My focus was on the innovation center recommendation so I did a case study on how Walmart successfully invested in startups to help their E-commerce division. Our team was able to present our recommendations to a group of Bosch executives in Chicago. I really enjoyed this experience and valued being able to practice my research and presentation skills. Our presenation can be seen here.
